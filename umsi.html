University of Michigan School of Information
Master's of Science in Information Candidate

The University of Michigan School of Information (UMSI) has provided a number of wonderful opportunities. In addition to serving as Treasurer for the School of Information Masters Association (SIMA), I have participated in the projects and activities highlighted below.

UMSI Design Clinic

I volunteered as an apprentice with the UMSI Design Clinic on a project for a local mechanical engineering company. Our team created a user interface for a new software system and took second place in the end-of-year Design Clinic award ceremony. I contributed significantly to brainstorming facilitation, information architecture, and wireframe creation.

Astronomy Archive

During the Fall 2017 semester I worked with three other students to create an online astronomy archive. The project required accessing resources at the U-M Bentley Library. We originally thought we could combine our interests in astronomy and cosmology, but soon learned that the Bentley did not hold sufficient materials on cosmology. Our focus then shifted to the history of astronomy at the University of Michigan and targeted high school and middle school teachers and students as users.

I contributed the majority of the imagery through research at the Bentley museum and online collection. I also created the “Directors of the Detroit Observatory” timeline and website wireframes. Our site's structure was limited by the Omeka software.

Information Architecture (IA) Summit—Poster Presentation

I learned about Laban Movement Analysis while auditing a class on Engineering Applications of Media Technology. The professor recommended we attend a lecture by a visiting music professor who had used Laban methodologies to evaluate musicianship. I was intrigued by the concept and reseached the topic for possible application in human-computer interaction. I learned that it is being used in the field of robotics and there is a growing awareness for the design of mixed reality and wearable computing. I presented a poster at the 2016 IA Summit in Atlanta, and received a travel grant from UMSI to attend.

Alternative Spring Break Photo Gallery

Each year the U-M School of Information arranges volunteer opportunities for Masters students during the week of Spring Break. In 2017 I joined the group traveling to Washington, DC where I and another student assisted the American Library Association with taking photos of the city for social media use.
